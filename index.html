== Instructions ==

A logic grid puzzle is a puzzle that is comprised of a list of clues and a grid. The grid will display a certain number of categories, and each category is only mapped to one other item in each other category!

Your task, if you are up for the challenge, is to use the clues given in the following puzzles on the left to uncover their answers! When you are done, click on the "check solution" button to check your answers!

Happy puzzle solving!


Puzzle #1 : Trees

It's a bright and sunny day at the Jurong Lake Gardens and young Kenny has taken notes on several trees that he found throughout a lovely stroll in the gardens! However, he has messed up his notes and now, cannot figure out what he has written! Can you help him decipher the notes that he has taken?

*** Clues ***

  1. The tree with spoon-shaped leaves is not poisonous.
  2. Of the tree with thick leaves and the tree that is used to make chairs, one of them is called the Hard Milkwood.
  3. The Indian Rubber Tree, which is not poisonous, is an ornamental plant with thick leaves.
  4. The Pong Pong is poisonous and is used to make rat poison.


Puzzle #2 : Edible Plants

Jennifer, a community gardener, is planning a get-together with her gardening buddies Megan and Rose. All three gardeners shared produce from their gardens and made food with each others' produce: can you figure out who bought and made what?

*** Clues ***

  1. The Chocolate Mint plant is neither used for stir-fries or soups.
  2. The Thai basil is used for stir-frying, but cannot be frozen.
  3. The plant that can be frozen also needs some water to survive.
  4. The plant that is used for soups is not stored in water or freezable.
  5. The plant that can be stir-fried needs plenty of sunlight to grow.


Puzzle #3 : Animals and Diets

There are many fauna species that reside within Jurong Lake Gardens; each of these species not only has their own habitat, but also their own diets. Can you use the clues given on the right hand side to figure out the habitats and the diets of each species?

*** Clues ***

  1. The monitor lizard lives in the wetlands.
  2. The white-breasted waterhen lives in the marshes.
  3. The common kingfisher lives in an aquatic habitat, but unlike the monitor lizard or the white-breasted waterhen.
  4. The plaintain squirrel eats nuts.
  5. Of the animal that lives in the rivers and the animal that eats mammals, one of them is the kingfisher.
  6. The monitor lizard is fond of eating small mammals.
  7. Of the white-breasted waterhen and the common kingfisher, one of them eats fish and the other lives in the marshes.
